What Is Embodied Energy?

The concept behind our sustainability analysis.

Embodied energy is the total amount of energy required to produce a product or structure, starting from the extraction of raw materials, through manufacturing, processing, transportation, and assembly. It represents the hidden energy cost involved before the product is actually used. Understanding embodied energy helps engineers evaluate environmental impact, compare materials, and design more sustainable and energy-efficient systems.

Alternate Materials

Lower-embodied-energy alternatives for each component to improve sustainability.

01 · 3D Printed Body (PLA)

Body Alternatives

Recycled PLA / Recycled Plastic Sheets — Uses less energy than virgin PLA and reduces plastic waste.
Lightweight Cardboard / Corrugated Sheet (laminated) — Very low embodied energy, biodegradable, suitable for indoor models.
Thin Plywood / MDF — Lower embodied energy than plastics and easy to fabricate.
02 · BO Motor (60 rpm)

Motor Alternatives

Low-power Micro DC Motors — Smaller size, lower material use, hence reduced embodied energy.
Gear Motor with Plastic Gearbox — Plastic gears have lower embodied energy than metal ones.
03 · L298N Motor Driver

Driver Alternatives

L293D Motor Driver IC — Smaller IC, less material usage, lower embodied energy.
Single-channel Motor Driver Module — If only one motor is needed, material and energy use is reduced.
04 · IR Sensor

Sensor Alternatives

Ultrasonic Sensor (shared module use) — One sensor can cover more area, reducing total components.
Reused / Salvaged IR Sensor — Reusing components almost eliminates new embodied energy.
05 · ESP32 Controller

Controller Alternatives

ESP8266 / Arduino Nano — Smaller board, fewer materials, lower embodied energy when Wi-Fi is not required.
Reused Development Board — Best option for sustainability — no new manufacturing energy required.
