Engineering Design 1

Launcher Activity — building stability through hands-on prototyping.

The Launcher Build

In this activity, we used sticks, a spoon, and rubber bands to build a stable stand that could hold a ball and help it hit a target when thrown. Our goal was to make the structure strong and balanced so that the ball could be launched accurately. We worked together to adjust the position of the sticks and tighten the rubber bands to make the stand stable. This activity taught us the importance of stability, design thinking, and teamwork while building a functional model.

🎯

Goal

Build a stable stand that can hold a ball and launch it accurately toward a target.

🧱

Materials

Wooden sticks (popsicle), a plastic spoon, and rubber bands — simple materials with real engineering constraints.

💡

Lessons Learned

Stability, design thinking, iterative adjustment, and the value of collaborative teamwork in physical prototyping.

Engineering Design 3

Pugh Chart — evaluating 4 alternative designs against weighted objectives.

The Pugh Chart is a structured design evaluation tool that compares multiple design alternatives against a datum (baseline) using weighted criteria. Each design is scored relative to the datum as better (+), worse (−), or the same (S). The weighted totals determine which design best meets the requirements.
