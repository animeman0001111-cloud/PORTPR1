Activity 01

Ice Breaking Activity

A team observation and communication challenge using engineering cards.

What We Did

When the cards were given to us, we first noticed the pictures on each card. One of our team members noticed the numbers on the cards, and we started arranging them in order — by which we came to know about the missing card and duplicate card.

Once we knew which were the duplicate cards, we started approaching other teams to see if they had cards to exchange with us. Two members from our team who were good at communication began approaching different teams, and we collected many cards.

Reflection

The ice breaking activity was interesting and helped our team work together. By carefully observing the cards, we noticed the numbers and arranged them to find the missing and duplicate cards. We then communicated with other teams to exchange the cards. This activity taught us the importance of good observation, communication, teamwork, and time management to complete a task successfully.

Case Study

Bangalore Sewage Problem

Understanding how rapid urban growth creates real engineering challenges.

1. Rapid Urbanization

The population of Bangalore has increased rapidly over the years due to migration and urban development. However, the city's infrastructure has not expanded at the same pace. Because of this, existing sewage systems are overloaded and unable to manage the large amount of waste produced every day, leading to untreated sewage overflowing into lakes, drains, and nearby water bodies.

2. Poor Drainage Network

Many parts of the city still do not have proper underground drainage systems. In some areas, the drainage networks are old, damaged, or poorly maintained. Additionally, illegal sewage connections often discharge wastewater directly into stormwater drains — meant only for rainwater — causing pollution and foul smell in the surroundings.

3. Health Hazards

Improper sewage management creates serious health risks for people living nearby. Contaminated water can spread water-borne diseases such as cholera, typhoid, and dysentery. Poor sanitation conditions especially affect low-income communities where access to clean water and proper sanitation facilities is limited.

Possible Solutions

The city needs to expand and modernize sewage treatment plants so that wastewater can be treated effectively. Treated wastewater can be reused for gardening, industrial use, and construction. The government should also strictly monitor and prevent illegal sewage connections, while public awareness and community participation are essential to maintain clean drains.

Reflection

Learning about the Bangalore sewage problem made me understand how rapid urban growth can create serious environmental and health issues if infrastructure does not develop at the same pace. It showed me the importance of proper sewage management, government regulations, and public responsibility. Sustainable solutions like wastewater treatment and reuse are necessary for the healthy development of a city.

Activity 02

Reverse Engineering Activity

Disassembling and analysing existing products to understand how they were designed and built.

What We Explored

Reverse engineering involves taking apart a finished product and working backwards to understand its design, components, and how it functions. This activity gave us a hands-on look at how engineers think when they encounter an existing system.

By examining the product's parts, materials, and assembly, we were able to understand the design decisions made by the original engineers — and consider how the product could potentially be improved.

Key Takeaway

Reverse engineering teaches us that every engineered product is the result of deliberate decisions. Understanding those decisions helps us become better designers and problem-solvers ourselves.
