Physical Implementation

A hands-on journey from raw components to a finished ladybug robot — built sprint by sprint using acrylic, motors, and creativity.

View Sprints Bill of Materials

Project Overview


• 3 Prototyping Sprints

• Ladybug-shaped robot body

• Acrylic chassis & linkage system

• Motor-driven legged mechanism

• Papier-mâché exterior shell

Prototyping Sprints

Iterative builds that refined the design from bare mechanism to finished robot.

Sprint 01

Mechanism Assembly

The first sprint focused on building and testing the core walking mechanism. Acrylic linkage arms, cam shafts, and leg components were assembled and validated for motion. The chassis was bare — the priority was proving the kinematic system worked.

Sprint 02

Body Form & Structure

A dome-shaped body was introduced over the mechanism using a blue moulded shell, sitting atop the acrylic leg assembly. This sprint established the overall silhouette of the robot and confirmed the structural fit between the body and the locomotion base.

Sprint 03

Surface Finishing

The final sprint brought the ladybug theme to life. The body was covered in red papier-mâché with black spots, and a separate black head piece with painted eyes was attached. The robot took on its full visual character while wiring was integrated for the motor connections.

Final Prototype

The completed ladybug robot — combining the acrylic walking mechanism with a hand-crafted papier-mâché body.

Body

Red papier-mâché dome with black polka-dot spots, modelled to resemble a ladybug shell. The texture gives the prototype a handcrafted, organic quality.

Head

A separate black papier-mâché head section with hand-painted white-and-black eyes, attached to the front of the chassis to complete the ladybug form.

Chassis & Legs

The underlying acrylic structure houses the cam shafts, linkage arms, and motor. The leg mechanism converts rotational motor motion into a walking gait across the four acrylic legs.

Wiring

Red and black power wires run from the motor assembly out through the base, connecting to the external power supply for actuation during demonstrations.
