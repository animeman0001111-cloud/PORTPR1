MSBS Calculation

Motor Selection and Battery System calculations — determining the required torque output and adapter specifications for the ladybug robot.

Motor Selection Adapter Selection

Calculation Summary


• Robot mass: 0.7 kg

• Required torque: 0.4367 Nm

• With safety factor: 8.01 kg·cm

• Battery: 2× 3.7V = 7.4V

• Adapter rating: 8.4V, 1–2A

Motor Selection

Step-by-step torque calculation to find the minimum motor torque required to drive the robot, with a safety factor applied.

Step 1 — Weight Force

Formula F = mg
Values F = 0.7 × 9.81
Result F = 6.867 N

Step 2 — Torque (Nm)

Formula T = F × r
Values T = 6.867 × 0.0636
Result T = 0.4367 Nm

Step 3 — Unit Conversion

Convert Nm → kg·cm
Values T = 0.4367 × 10.197
Result T = 4.45 kg·cm

Step 4 — Safety Factor

Factor Safety Factor = 1.8
Values 1.8 × 4.45
Result = 8.01 kg·cm

Calculation Walkthrough

The reasoning behind each step in the motor selection process.

Weight Force (F = mg)

The total weight of the robot (0.7 kg) is multiplied by gravitational acceleration (9.81 m/s²) to find the force the motor must overcome — 6.867 N. This is the load the leg mechanism must support and move.

Torque (T = F × r)

Torque is calculated by multiplying the force by the effective radius of the cam shaft (0.0636 m). This gives the raw torque requirement of 0.4367 Nm — the minimum the motor shaft must deliver at the pivot point.

Unit Conversion to kg·cm

Motor torque ratings are commonly expressed in kg·cm rather than Nm. Converting using the factor 10.197 gives 4.45 kg·cm, making it directly comparable to motor datasheets.

Safety Factor of 1.8

A safety factor of 1.8 is applied to account for real-world variables — friction, load variations, and motor inefficiency. The final motor selection criterion is 8.01 kg·cm, meaning a motor rated at or above this torque is required.

Adapter Selection

Battery and charger specifications for the robot's power system, using two series-connected 3.7V Li-ion cells.

3.7V

Per Cell Voltage

Each Li-ion battery cell operates at a nominal voltage of 3.7V.

7.4V

Normal Voltage

Two 3.7V cells connected in series give a nominal operating voltage of 7.4V.

8.4V

Fully Charged

At full charge, two Li-ion cells in series reach 8.4V — the maximum safe voltage for this pack.

1–2A

Charger Current Rating

The charger/adapter must output 8.4V at 1A to 2A to safely charge the 2200 mAh battery pack.

2200

Battery Capacity (mAh)

Each cell has a capacity of 2200 mAh, providing adequate runtime for the robot's motor and electronics.

Adapter Spec

Required adapter: input 7.4V nominal, output 8.4V at 1–2A. Example: a standard 2S Li-ion balance charger.
