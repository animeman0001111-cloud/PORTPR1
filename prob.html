Problem Definition

Designing a ladybug-inspired Insectronics system for safe indoor navigation, obstacle avoidance, and environmental awareness.

Version 1.1 Final Version

Design Constraints 🐞


• Size: 18–20 cm

• Weight: < 2 kg

• Voltage: 5–12 V

• Budget: ≤ ₹5,000

• Application: Indoor use

Version 1.1

The initial problem statement establishing the Insectronics ladybug concept.

Design a ladybug-inspired model as an Insectronics system to achieve safe indoor navigation and basic environmental awareness with clear visual indication of object detection and battery status using LEDs. The model is limited to a compact size of 18–20 cm, a total weight below 2 kg, an operating voltage range of 5–12 V, and a total cost not exceeding ₹5,000, ensuring energy efficiency, affordability, and suitability for indoor use. The system moves forward, senses obstacles and turns left or right to avoid collisions, monitors temperature and air quality, and provides system status indication through LEDs, rotates its head or sensor unit to scan surroundings, measures the distance to nearby objects, and displays battery level information using an LED indicator.

List of Objectives

What the Insectronics system is designed to achieve.

Reduce Crop Damage

Detect and monitor harmful insects in farms to help reduce agricultural losses for small and medium-scale farmers.

Wide-Area Scanning

Allow the robot to scan a wider area for objects by rotating its sensor unit.

Instant Status Feedback

Quickly inform users about object detection and battery status through clear LED indications.

Safe Collision Avoidance

Help the robot detect and avoid obstacles, enabling safe and reliable autonomous movement.

Continuous Monitoring

Monitor surroundings continuously without requiring manual control or constant user input.

Energy Efficiency

Allow longer operation with limited battery power through optimised low-power design.

General Accessibility

Make the system accessible for general users and small-scale applications within a ₹5,000 budget.

Future Upgradability

Support future upgrades to improve insect detection accuracy and add advanced smart features.

Version 1.2

A refined and more detailed problem statement incorporating objectives and constraints.

To develop a compact ladybug-inspired Insectronics model capable of safe indoor navigation and basic environmental monitoring. The system is designed to autonomously move forward, detect obstacles, and change direction to prevent collisions while maintaining visual feedback through LEDs for object detection and battery status. Operating within a size of 18–20 cm, a weight under 2 kg, a voltage range of 5–12 V, and a cost limit of ₹5,000, the model emphasises energy efficiency, affordability, and indoor usability. Additional features include head or sensor rotation for environmental scanning, distance measurement to nearby objects, temperature and air quality monitoring, and LED-based battery level indication.

List of Constraints

Design boundaries and operational requirements the system must respect.

🐛

Insect Focus

Detection limited to harmful insects only — such as locusts and beetles — not all insect types.

☀

Solar Power

Powered by solar energy to suit farm environments where reliable electricity may not be available.

🌾

Outdoor Field Use

Designed for deployment in outdoor farm fields, requiring durability and weather resilience.

💰

Farmer Affordability

Must be affordable for small and medium-scale farmers, keeping total cost within ₹5,000.

⏳

3–5 Year Lifespan

Expected to operate reliably for at least three to five years with minimal degradation.

🔧

Low Maintenance

Requires minimal servicing — preferably maintenance only once every six months.

Version 1.3 — Final

The complete, finalised problem definition for the Insectronics model.

The objective is to design and implement a ladybug-inspired Insectronics system that enables intelligent indoor movement and environmental awareness. The model navigates autonomously by moving forward, sensing obstacles, and turning left or right to avoid collisions, while providing clear LED indications for obstacle detection and battery status. Constrained to a compact form factor of 18–20 cm, a total weight below 2 kg, an operating voltage of 5–12 V, and a maximum budget of ₹5,000, the system focuses on low power consumption and cost-effectiveness. It also incorporates a rotating sensor mechanism for surrounding scans, distance measurement, temperature and air quality sensing, and real-time battery level display using LEDs.

List of Functions

Key functional capabilities the completed system must provide.

Detect & Act

The system detects harmful insects first, then automatically activates a safe, appropriate control response.

Smart Alerts

Sends real-time alerts via SMS or a mobile application when threats or anomalies are detected.

Manual Override

Farmers retain a manual override option to take direct control of the robot at any time.

Multi-Mode Alarms

Issues both sound alarms and mobile push notifications to ensure users are always informed.

Location Tracking

Basic location tracking allows users to monitor the robot's position within the field or space.
